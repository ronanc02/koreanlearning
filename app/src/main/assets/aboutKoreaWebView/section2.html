South Korea in numbers

Regime: republic with a legislative chamber

Capital: Seoul

Area: 97,100 km², roughly the size of French Guiana

Highest point: Mount Halla (1,950 m)

Population: 51.6 million (World Bank, 2018)

Life expectancy: 82.6 years (World Bank, 2017)

Literacy rate: 98% (MAE, 2016)

GDP per capita: 31,362.8 US dollars (World Bank, 2018) – for comparison, that of France is 41,463.6 US dollars (World Bank, 2018)

Official language: Korean

Religions: no religion (57%), Buddhism (15.5%), Protestantism (19.7%), Catholicism (7.9%), Confucianism, shamanism, Islam (Office National Statistics, 2015)

UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Baekje Historic Areas, Changdeokgung Palace Complex, Hwaseong Fortress, Seokguram Cave and Bulguksa Temple, Namhansanseong, Jongmyo Shrine, Sansa: Buddhist Mountain Monasteries in Korea, Dolmen Sites of Gochang, Hwasun and Ganghwa, Haeinsa Janggyeong Temple, Royal Tombs of Joseon Dynasty, Historic Villages of Korea: Hahoe and Yangdong, Historic Areas of Gyeongju, Volcanic Island and Lava Tunnels from Jeju
